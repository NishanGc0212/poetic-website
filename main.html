Her

She walked by like a season that never asked to stay just left warmth on everything quiet. I said nothing. But the air did. The curtains moved even when the wind didn’t. She saw me once— through the stairs, and ran. She never knew that from that very moment, I began to love her. Not loudly. Just like clocks love time always, but never asking why. I never said a word. Not because I didn’t want to… but because I didn’t know how. What if she said no? What if I lost even her shadow? So I kept it folded in the hush between her footsteps and mine. But now, maybe now, I will tell her. Because love like this was never meant to be buried in fear.
